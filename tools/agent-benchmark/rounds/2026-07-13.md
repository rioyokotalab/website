# Benchmark round — 2026-07-13

## Decision

Promote progressive disclosure, task-specific low-effort routing,
schema-free runner-lite capture, focused inspection for explicit local text
edits, compact decision output, and quiet browser serving. Keep durable
handoffs for lookup, long, incremental, or interruption-prone work; keep broad
inspection for visual work and diagnosis.

39,736 effective tokens; held-out WBD-005 used 28,359, for 68,095 combined.

the conservative same-version WBD-001/002/003 comparison is the decision-grade

## Rejected or corrected variants

- A redundant structured final schema increased effective tokens 47.8%.
- Naive bounded inspection caused a CRLF scope failure.
- Focused inspection was unsafe for reference-driven visual CSS.
- Repeated deterministic misses exposed underspecified task/grader contracts;
  they were corrected and versioned instead of escalating model strength.
- Held-out v1 was invalidated by authorization and grader invariants, not
  counted as a capability result, and retained in append-only evidence.

## Spend and next-round gate

Setup used 1,187,318 effective tokens over 53 instrumented runs; 292,490
(24.6%) were failed-run spend. The final stop/audit rules would have avoided at
least 130,007. A new round requires a concrete change or demand-backed coverage
gap, zero-token preflight, one probe with at least 10% preliminary improvement,
and a planned tuning budget below 25% of expected savings over 20 comparable
tasks. Do not reuse the exposed held-out task to tune routing.

Known limits: the suite is repository-specific, the final frozen tasks each
have one run, visual coverage has one mobile viewport, and root manual review
duration is unavailable to the runtime.
